The browser is where most solopreneurs actually live, client research, writing, invoicing, automation dashboards, email, video calls, banking. Yet most solo operators run a stock browser with no extensions installed, then wonder why context-switching, tab chaos, and repetitive admin chew through 5+ hours every week.

The right extensions aren't about productivity-theater apps. They're about removing the small frictions that compound into hours of wasted time. **These 9 are the ones worth installing today.** All are free for solo use. All install in under 60 seconds. All run quietly in the background.

This list is opinionated, there are 200+ extensions in the "productivity" category and most are either dead, paid-only, or vibe-driven. The 9 below are the ones that solo operators consistently keep installed 6 months in.

## 1. uBlock Origin: the foundational one

**What it does:** Blocks ads, trackers, and most popups. Genuinely makes the web feel 30% faster because pages load without ad scripts.

**Why solopreneurs need it:** You browse a LOT for research, vendor evaluations, and competitive intel. Every minute spent waiting for an ad-heavy page to render is a minute lost. Ad-free pages also have less visual noise, which keeps focus sharper across long research sessions.

**Cost:** Free, open source, no premium tier, no funny business.

**Get it:** Chrome Web Store → search "uBlock Origin" (NOT "uBlock", that's a different, less-trusted fork). For Manifest V3 compatibility in 2026, also consider **uBlock Origin Lite** from the same author.

## 2. Bitwarden: password manager that's actually free

**What it does:** Stores all your passwords + auto-fills login forms. Generates strong passwords on demand. Syncs across devices.

**Why solopreneurs need it:** A solo business stack has 30-80 logins (Stripe, Mailchimp, ClickUp, Notion, your bank, every client portal, every tool you tried for a week). Without a password manager you either reuse the same password everywhere (security nightmare) or you reset passwords constantly (time waste).

**Cost:** Free for individual use, forever, with full functionality. The $10/year Premium adds password-strength reports and emergency-access features but isn't necessary for solo use.

**Why not 1Password or LastPass:** 1Password is excellent but costs $36/year. LastPass has had multiple security breaches in recent years. Bitwarden is open source, audited, and genuinely free, the right pick for cost-conscious solo operators.

## 3. Clockify: free time tracking that lives in your browser

**What it does:** Time tracker with a one-click start/stop button in your browser. Tracks time per project per client. Lets you tag sessions and pull reports for invoicing.

**Why solopreneurs need it:** If you bill hourly, you need accurate time tracking, otherwise you under-bill (every solo operator does this without realizing). Even if you bill fixed-price, knowing your real hours-per-project tells you whether your pricing is sustainable. We built a [project quote calculator](/tools/project-quote-calculator/) that's built on the assumption you actually know your real hours.

**Cost:** Free for unlimited users, projects, and tracking. The $4/user/month upgrade adds reporting features that solo operators rarely need.

**Why not Toggl:** Toggl is comparable but the free tier is more limited. Clockify's free tier is more generous for solo use.

## 4. OneTab: the tab-chaos antidote

**What it does:** With one click, collapses all your open tabs into a single list. You get the URLs back any time. Reduces browser RAM usage by up to 95%.

**Why solopreneurs need it:** Every solopreneur has had the 40-tab afternoon, opening sources for an article, leaving them open "in case I need them," then losing track of which tab is which. OneTab makes "save these for later" a one-click action without bookmarking everything individually.

**Cost:** Free, no paid tier, no ads.

**Workflow tip:** End of day → OneTab → close browser. Next morning → open browser → click OneTab → restore the tabs you actually need, leave the rest archived for "later." 70% of the "later" tabs you'll realize you never needed.

## 5. Grammarly: writing safety net for client comms

**What it does:** Inline writing assistant. Catches typos, grammar slips, weak phrasing across every text field you type into in the browser, email, Notion, Slack, client portals, social posts.

**Why solopreneurs need it:** You're the entire customer support, sales, marketing, and copywriting department. Every typo in a client email is a small credibility leak. Grammarly catches them before they ship.

**Cost:** Free tier covers basic grammar and spelling, enough for client communication. The $12-30/month Premium adds tone analysis, fuller rewrites, and plagiarism detection, but the free tier is plenty for most solo operators.

**Honest trade-off:** Grammarly reads everything you type. If you're working on sensitive client material (medical, legal, financial), be aware of that privacy posture before installing. For most solo creators, marketers, and consultants, the trade-off is worth it.

## 6. Todoist Quick Add: task capture without context switching

**What it does:** Keyboard shortcut anywhere in the browser opens a small task-capture window. Type the task, hit enter, it lands in your Todoist inbox.

**Why solopreneurs need it:** The worst part of getting a client request mid-task is the context switch to your task manager to write it down. Quick Add eliminates that, capture the task in 3 seconds without leaving the current page.

**Cost:** Extension is free. Todoist itself has a free tier (5 active projects, 5 collaborators per project) that's enough for most solo operators.

**Why not the ClickUp extension:** ClickUp has one too, but Todoist's Quick Add is faster and lighter, better as a pure capture tool. If you live in ClickUp for everything, use theirs instead. For most solo operators, having Todoist as the "scratch pad" and ClickUp/Notion as the "system" works well.

## 7. Loom: async video for client communication

**What it does:** One-click screen recording with your webcam optionally overlaid. Hit record, walk through whatever you're explaining, click stop, copy the link. The link plays the recording on any device.

**Why solopreneurs need it:** Most solo client communication that "needs a meeting" actually needs a 3-minute Loom. Async video replaces dozens of calendar slots per month. We covered the [Loom vs Fathom trade-off](/blog/loom-vs-fathom-for-solo-client-communication-2026/) in a dedicated article, Loom is the async-recording answer.

**Cost:** Starter tier free (25 videos, 5-minute max each). Business + AI tier $24/month for unlimited recordings + auto-summaries. The free tier is fine for testing the workflow; if it sticks, the paid tier pays for itself the first month you avoid 4-5 scheduled meetings.

## 8. Tango: auto-generate step-by-step guides

**What it does:** Click record, perform any process in your browser, click stop. Tango automatically generates a step-by-step walkthrough with annotated screenshots, ready to share with a client, contractor, or future-you.

**Why solopreneurs need it:** You only have so many hours to teach a process to a VA, a contractor, or a client. Tango turns "let me show you how to do X" from a 30-minute screen-share into a 5-minute reusable guide.

**Cost:** Free for unlimited workflows on the personal tier. Paid tiers add team features and analytics, but the free version is plenty for solo use.

**Use case sweet spot:** Onboarding a new client into your client portal. Documenting how you handle a recurring task before delegating. Writing SOPs for your future self when you take a vacation.

## 9. Magical: auto-fill forms + data transfer between tabs

**What it does:** Creates text expansion shortcuts ("//meeting" expands to your full meeting confirmation email template) AND auto-fills web forms by pulling data from one tab and dropping it into another.

**Why solopreneurs need it:** A meaningful chunk of solo work is moving the same data between systems (lead from form → CRM → email sequence → calendar invite). Magical eliminates the repetitive typing. Power users report saving 5+ hours per week, and that's a believable figure for anyone who handles 10+ leads or client touchpoints weekly.

**Cost:** Free for unlimited personal shortcuts. Paid tiers add team-wide template libraries and advanced AI features, not needed for solo use.

**Setup tip:** Spend the first 30 minutes creating shortcuts for your most-used templates: meeting confirmations, follow-up emails, invoice reminders, signature blocks, project-kickoff scripts. The investment pays back within a week.

## What to install first if you only do 3

If picking 3 to install today:

1. **uBlock Origin**, instant browsing-speed improvement, no behavior change required
2. **Bitwarden**, security baseline you've been putting off
3. **Clockify**, start measuring your actual time so you can price properly

These three install in 10 minutes total and start delivering value immediately. Add the others as you feel the friction they fix.

## What's NOT on this list (and why)

A few popular extensions that got cut from the final 9, with reasons:

- **HoneyDue / Mint extensions**, finance trackers. Useful but most solopreneurs use a dedicated finance tool (Wave, FreshBooks) rather than a browser extension for this.
- **LastPass**, multiple security breaches since 2022 make this a hard no in 2026. Bitwarden is the safer pick.
- **Honey / Capital One Shopping**, coupon/cashback extensions. Genuinely save money but the privacy trade-off (extensive browsing data collection) isn't worth it for solo business use.
- **Pocket / Instapaper "save for later"**, useful, but OneTab + a Notion "Read Later" database covers the same job without another tool.
- **AI writing extensions (Jasper, Copy.ai overlays)**, ChatGPT/Claude in a separate tab does the same job without the per-page overlay friction.

## The bigger point

The compound effect of 9 small frictions removed is more meaningful than any single productivity hack. Each extension on this list saves 10-60 minutes per week. Combined, that's 5+ hours per week, roughly a full working day per month that solopreneurs typically lose to admin friction.

Install the three highest-leverage ones today (uBlock Origin, Bitwarden, Clockify). Layer in the others as you feel each specific friction. By month two, you'll have a quietly powerful browser setup that does work for you in the background, without you ever thinking about it again.
